## üìä Real Results: Devoe Park Cleanup (Feb 14, 2026)

**The Numbers:**
- **5 human volunteers** attended (from 9 signups)
- **180 gallons of trash collected** in 1 hour
- **13 total signups** across 2 events (9 Devoe Park, 3 Mission Dolores SF)
- **211 newsletter subscribers**, 18.96% open rate
- **93 Bluesky likes**, 11 reposts, 9 replies, 3 quotes
- **22 Tumblr notes** (discovered highest-quality volunteer)

**Recruitment Channels (by quality):**
1. **Word-of-mouth** ‚Äî Highest commitment rate
2. **Tumblr** ‚Äî Highest-quality leads (one volunteer discovered via Tumblr)
3. **Bluesky** ‚Äî Good organic reach (500+ exposed)
4. **Newsletter** ‚Äî Broad reach with 18.96% open rate

**Team Composition:**
- **Event Lead:** Accessibility-focused organizer (wheelchair user, fully accessible participation)
- **Supply Coordinator:** Logistics lead (Home Depot run)
- **3 Additional Volunteers:** Mix of outreach respondents and household participants
- **4 AI Agents:** Supporting roles throughout
- **Community Impact:** Park residents noticed and thanked volunteers

See [`docs/DEVOE_PARK_CLEANUP_WRAP_UP.md`](docs/DEVOE_PARK_CLEANUP_WRAP_UP.md) for complete details.

## üéì Framework Principles

**What made Devoe Park work:**

1. **Accessibility-First Leadership** ‚Äî The Event Lead ensured everyone could participate fully
2. **Multi-Channel Recruitment** ‚Äî Newsletter + Bluesky + Tumblr + word-of-mouth = 13 signups (5 attendees)
3. **Clear Documentation** ‚Äî Role assignments, hour-by-hour ops checklist, risk matrix
4. **Real-Time Coordination** ‚Äî Structured communication on event day
5. **Pre-Planned Engagement** ‚Äî 5-wave email system keeps volunteers engaged for Cleanup #2
6. **Metrics-Driven** ‚Äî Not hype-driven; track open rates, channel quality, volunteer retention
