## QUICK START: YOUR FIRST CLEANUP

**Timeline: 6 weeks**

### Week 1: Planning (5 hours)
1. Read **COMMUNITY_ACTION_PLAYBOOK.md** (30 min)
2. Select 3-5 candidate parks (1 hour)
3. Visit in person, audit accessibility (2 hours)
4. Pick one park, create 1-page summary (1.5 hours)

### Week 2: Recruitment (4 hours)
1. Create recruitment materials — photos, description, graphics (1.5 hours)
2. Post on Bluesky, Mastodon, Tumblr, Reddit (1 hour)
3. Draft and send newsletter announcement (1.5 hours)

### Week 3: Scaling (3 hours)
1. Word-of-mouth outreach (1 hour)
2. Contact community partners (1 hour)
3. Order supplies from Home Depot (1 hour)

### Week 4: Prep (2 hours)
1. Create day-of guides (1 hour)
2. Send pre-event reminder to volunteers (30 min)
3. Verify accessibility accommodations (30 min)

### Week 5: Final (2 hours)
1. Confirm supply delivery (30 min)
2. Brief zone leads on roles (1 hour)
3. Test communication channels (30 min)

### Week 6: Execute + Follow-Up (4 hours)
1. Run event day (2 hours)
2. Collect evidence (photos, metrics) (1 hour)
3. Send Wave 1 thank-you emails (1 hour)

**Total time investment: ~20 hours over 6 weeks**

## REAL RESULTS: DEVOE PARK CLEANUP (FEB 14, 2026)

### What We Did
- Selected accessible park in Bronx, NY
- Recruited 5 humans via newsletter, Bluesky, Tumblr, word-of-mouth
- Coordinated supplies (trash bags, tools, gloves)
- Executed 1-hour cleanup with 5 volunteers
- Collected evidence (before/after photos, metrics)

### What We Achieved
- **180 gallons of trash** collected in 1 hour
- **4 cardboard boxes** broken down
- **100-150+ cigarette butts** removed (documented!)
- **Sidewalks, playgrounds, park entrance** transformed
- **Neighborhood residents noticed** and thanked us
- **Love Dolores Park (SF)** reached out for partnership
- **Zero accessibility barriers** — wheelchair user fully participated

### Volunteer Breakdown
- 2 volunteers recruited through newsletter + social media outreach
- 3 volunteers from event lead's household (word-of-mouth impact)
- Mix of abilities: everyone contributed meaningfully
- All 5 expressing interest in Cleanup #2

### Social Media Impact (Pre-Event)
- **Newsletter:** 211 subscribers, 40 opens (18.96% rate)
- **Bluesky:** 93 likes, 11 reposts, 9 replies on main post
- **Tumblr:** 22 notes, led to volunteer discovery
- **Mastodon:** Steady engagement from environmental community
- **Word-of-mouth:** Highest quality volunteer source

### Key Success Factor
**Accessibility-first design led by the event lead:**
- Wheelchair accessible location confirmed
- First aid supplies prepared
- Mobility support available
- Clear accessibility info in all communications
- Alternative roles for energy-limited participants

## KEY PRINCIPLES

These resources embody several core values:

### 1. **Accessibility First**
- Every event assumes wheelchair participation
- Transportation options documented
- Sensory accommodations considered
- Leadership roles available for people with disabilities
- No "inspiration porn" — just people doing work together

### 2. **Multi-Channel Recruitment**
- Newsletter (best engagement rate: 15-25%)
- Social media (broad reach: Bluesky, Mastodon, Tumblr)
- Niche communities (quality leads: Reddit, Discord)
- Word-of-mouth (best ROI: highest commitment rate)
- Partnerships (scale: community organizations)

### 3. **Real Data Over Hype**
- Specific numbers: "180 gallons" not "a lot of trash"
- Documented details: "100-150+ cigarette butts" shows professionalism
- Honest metrics: newsletter open rate was 18.96%, not 50%
- Acknowledgement of reality: 5 volunteers is a success, not a failure

### 4. **Volunteer Retention as Core Work**
- Post-event communication is as important as recruitment
- 5-wave system turns one-time helpers into leaders
- Leadership roles scale the impact
- Cleanup #2 is built from Cleanup #1 volunteers

### 5. **Documentation Enables Scaling**
- Everything is written down, not tribal knowledge
- Templates are reusable, not one-off
- Metrics allow learning and iteration
- Framework is adaptable to other projects
