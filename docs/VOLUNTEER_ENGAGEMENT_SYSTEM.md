After the Devoe Park cleanup, you have a critical choice: treat volunteers as one-time helpers or invest in turning them into long-term community leaders.

The 5-Wave Email Strategy takes volunteers on a journey:

1. **Wave 1 (Day 1):** "Thank you for showing up" â†’ Build emotional connection

5. **Wave 5 (Day 14):** "You're building something bigger" â†’ Convert to next event

## KEY SUCCESS FACTORS

1. **Use Real Data**
   - Real supply counts (180 gallons, not "many bags")
   - Real volunteer names and contributions
   - Real photos and evidence
   - Real community feedback ("locals said hello")

2. **Personalize Every Email**
   - One email per volunteer (not mass BCC)
   - Reference their specific role/zone
   - Mention what they personally accomplished
   - Use their first name

3. **Create Clear CTAs (Calls-to-Action)**
   - What should they do after reading?
   - Reply with preference / vote / refer a friend
   - Make it easy (one reply, not three forms)
   - Acknowledge small commitments

4. **Tell Stories, Not Just Metrics**
   - "Trash had been sitting for months" (concrete detail)
   - "Locals said hello" (community impact)
   - "Wheelchair doubled as mobile supply station" (creative problem-solving)
   - Volunteer quotes and reflections

5. **Escalate Gradually**
   - Wave 1: Light touch (just say thanks)
   - Waves 2-3: Invite deepening (involvement poll)
   - Wave 4: Formalize leadership (concrete role offer)
   - Wave 5: Movement building (Cleanup #2 planning)
